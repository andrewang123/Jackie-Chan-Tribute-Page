Jackie Chan Tribute Page

Jackie Chan

Martial Artist, Actor, Film Director,
Producer, Stuntman, Singer

Jackie revolutionized martial arts filmography
by adding his own comedic twist.

Here's a brief timeline on Jackie Chan's
achievements as of 2016:

  • 1954: Born on April 7th in Hong Kong, China. Jackie's birth name was Chan Kong-sang. He was given the name Pao-Pao (Cannonball) because he was always energetic and rolling around.
  • 1961: September 4th, Jackie enrolls in Peking Opera School. As he walked into the school he saw kids somersaulting and playing with swords and sticks, which launched his passion for acting.
  • 1961: Jackie’s father takes a job as head cook at the American embassy in Australia, and enrolls his son at Master Yu’s China Drama Academy on Kowloon Island. He learns martial arts, acrobatics, singing and acting there. Chan will live at the academy for the next ten years.
  • 1962: On October 6th, Jackie appeared in his 1st movie "Big And Little Wong Tin Bar."
  • 1969: Jackie leaves the academy to become a stuntman.
  • 1973: Bruce Lee, one of Jackie's inspiration passed away. Jackie went on to follow in his footsteps, but adding his own comedic twist.
  • 1976: Got his break from director-producer Lo Wei, who signed and put him in the lead role in "Shaolin Wooden Men", "The Killer Meteors", "New Fist of Fury", "To Kill With Intrigue" and the early John Woo film "Hand of Death."
  • 1979: Xiao quan guai zhao (The Fearless Hyena) marks Jackie’s directorial debut.
  • 1980: Jackie heads to Hollywood to purse his acting career. His first acting role in Hollywood was in the film called "The Big Brawl".
  • 1980: Directs and stars in "The Young Master", the 10th anniversary of the studio's founding which became the year's highest-grossing film in Hong Kong with $11 million Hong Kong dollars.
  • 1981: Jackie played a minor role in the 1981 film The Cannonball Run, which grossed $100 million worldwide. It's' during this time where he came to a fascination in outtakes shown in the credits.
  • 1982: Marries Lin Feng-Jiao (Joan Lin). They married in Los Angeles, California.
  • 1982: Jackie's first son Jaycee Chan is born in Los Angeles California.
  • 1985: Produced and directed and starred in "Police Story", which was a Hollywood influenced action-comedy. The movie received the Best Film award at the Hong Kong Film Awards.
  • 1985: Breaks two vertebrae in his spine while performing a stunt for Police Story.
  • 1995: Rumble in the Bronx provides Jackie with his breakthrough film in the United States.
  • 1998: Rush Hour becomes his first major box office success in the States.
  • 1999: Jackie wins the Hollywood Film Award: Actor of the year.
  • 2002: April 10th, Jackie receives a Walk of Fame star on Hollywood Boulevard.
  • 2002: Was nominated for a Daytime Emmy Award: Outstanding Performer in an Animated Program Jackie Chan Adventures (2000).
  • 2002: Was nominated for the Blimp Award: Favorite Male Movie Star and won the Favorite Male Action Hero award for Rush Hour 2 (2001)
  • 2003: Frustrated by the lack of suitable roles in Hollywood, Jackie establishes his own production company, JCE Movies Limited (Jackie Chan Emperor Movies Limited) in association with Emperor Multimedia Group.
  • 2004: Launches his own line of clothing called Jackie Chan Design. His website varies in products from car accessories, to clothing, to kitchenware.
  • 2007: Records We Are Ready, the official countdown song to the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games.
  • 2010: Flies to Tongren City in the Guizhou province of China to deliver 48,000 bottles of water to the drought-stricken inhabitants.
  • 2011: Announces that he and other Hong Kong stars will stage a charity concert in April to raise money for victims of the recent Japanese earthquake and tsunami.
  • 2011: Won Kid's Choice Award Favourite Butt Kicker for The Karate Kid (2010) and People Choice Awards: Favorite Action Star
  • 2012: At the launch of Chinese Zodiac at the Cannes Film Festival, Jackie announces his retirement from making action movies.
  • 2012: Later that year, Jackie changes his mind over his decision to retire from action movies.
  • 2012: Sets a record for the most stunts in a movie by a living actor and most credits in one movie, Chinese Zodiac (2012).
  • 2016: Jackie's net worth is $365 million at the age of 63.
